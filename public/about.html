James Calvano

Hoboken, New Jersey

Hi! My name is James Calvano, I am a technologist interested in business and economics. In my free time I enjoy exploring new places, learning about other cultures, continuously learning and getting outside.

My interest in technology started from a young age when my family wanted to share one printer. By using the printer manufacturers's website I researched how to attach the family printer to our home network. Excited after completing that project, my interest later evolved into computer networking (routing/firewalls). Later, I was able to apply this interest by being able to network a few Wi-Fi access points spread throughout my family's home to expand coverage for a growing number of devices. A few years later after being given my first computer in the eighth grade I developed an interest in web development. I followed web-dev/graphic design YouTubers that covered concepts from programming to design.

The only thing to match my interest in technology was that of business. My father is a small business owner so daily I was a witness of the benefits as well as the costs of entrepreneurship. In the same vein of business, when I was in middle school I joined the Stock Market Club where my small team was given a simulated $100k to invest. Somehow by typing in random characters (stock tickers) into Yahoo Finance then "buying" share of the companies with the coolest names my team won the game and made a leaderboard in our area's local newspaper. Years later when I was sixteen I convinced my Mom to open a UTMA account with some of my life savings. Also around the age of sixteen I had taken AP Economics; there I learned concepts like supply/demand, market factors (labor/supply) and opportunity cost. Witnessing an entrepreneur deliever value, fasinated by markets and thinking at the time economics could explain everything, I knew I wanted to study business in college. It was not until a few years into my career that I was able to bring both my interests in technology and business together.

I attended the University of Dayton and graduated with a B.S.B.A. in Finance and Economics with an emphasis in Investment Management.
